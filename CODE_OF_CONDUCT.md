# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Be respectful** – Treat all participants with respect and consideration
- **Be inclusive** – Use welcoming and inclusive language
- **Be constructive** – Provide constructive feedback and accept it gracefully
- **Be collaborative** – Work together toward common goals
- **Be professional** – Maintain professionalism in all interactions
- **Assume good intent** – Give others the benefit of the doubt
- **Focus on facts** – Keep discussions centered on code and technical merit
- **Be patient** – Remember that people have varying levels of experience
- **Give credit** – Acknowledge others' contributions and ideas

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment** – Offensive comments, intimidation, stalking, or unwelcome attention
- **Discrimination** – Prejudice or exclusion based on protected characteristics
- **Hate speech** – Derogatory language targeting individuals or groups
- **Trolling** – Deliberately inflammatory or disruptive comments
- **Personal attacks** – Ad hominem arguments or insults
- **Doxxing** – Publishing private information without consent
- **Sexual harassment** – Unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- **Microaggressions** – Subtle but harmful comments or behaviors
- **Plagiarism** – Claiming others' work as your own
- **Academic dishonesty** – Violating academic integrity policies

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including:

- GitHub repository (issues, PRs, discussions, code)
- Communication channels (email, chat, video calls)
- Project meetings and events
- Social media when representing the project
- Any public or private space when representing the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it promptly:

**Preferred methods:**
1. **GitHub:** Contact @wmoore012 via direct message
2. **Email:** [Add contact email]
3. **For sensitive/security issues:** See [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md)

**What to include in your report:**
- Your contact information
- Names of individuals involved
- Description of the incident
- Date, time, and location (if applicable)
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, logs, witnesses)
- Whether you've reported this elsewhere

**Confidentiality:** All reports will be handled with discretion. We will protect the identity of reporters to the extent possible.

### Enforcement Process

1. **Receipt acknowledgment** – Within 2 business days
2. **Investigation** – Gather facts from all parties
3. **Decision** – Determine if Code of Conduct was violated
4. **Action** – Apply appropriate consequences
5. **Follow-up** – Check in with reporter and involved parties

### Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences:

#### 1. Correction (Minor Violation)

**Community Impact:** Inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence:** Private written warning with clarity on the violation and explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

#### 2. Warning (Moderate Violation)

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** Warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces and external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

#### 3. Temporary Ban (Serious Violation)

**Community Impact:** Serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** Temporary ban from any interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

#### 4. Permanent Ban (Severe Violation)

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation, harassment, or aggression toward individuals or groups.

**Consequence:** Permanent ban from any public interaction within the community.

### Appeals

If you believe an enforcement decision was made in error, you may appeal by:

1. Emailing the project lead within 7 days
2. Providing new information or context
3. Explaining why you believe the decision was incorrect

Appeals will be reviewed by maintainers not involved in the original decision.

## Academic & Professional Context

### Special Considerations

This project serves dual purposes:

1. **Academic project** – M.S. Data Science course deliverable
2. **Professional MVP** – Potential real-world application

**Additional expectations:**

- **Academic integrity** – Follow your institution's honor code and collaboration policies
- **Professional conduct** – Maintain standards expected in industry settings
- **Instructor visibility** – Course instructors may review project interactions
- **Company stakeholders** – Potential company contacts may observe the project

**Academic violations** (plagiarism, cheating, unauthorized collaboration) may be reported to:
- Course instructors
- Academic integrity offices
- University administration (as required)

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying standards of acceptable behavior
- Taking appropriate and fair corrective action
- Removing, editing, or rejecting contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- Communicating reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate
- Leading by example in all interactions

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct may face temporary or permanent repercussions.
